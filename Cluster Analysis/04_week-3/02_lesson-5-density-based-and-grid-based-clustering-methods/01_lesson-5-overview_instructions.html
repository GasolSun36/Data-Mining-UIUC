Lesson 5: Density-Based and Grid-Based Clustering Methods

Overview

In this lesson, you will learn density-based clustering and grid-based clustering methods. We will introduce the basic concepts of density-based clustering and some typical density-based clustering methods such as DBSCAN and OPTICS. Also, we will introduce the basic concepts of grid-based clustering and a few typical grid-based clustering methods, such as STING and CLIQUE.

Time

This lesson should take approximately two hours of dedicated time to complete, with its videos and assignments.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Understand the basic concepts of density-based clustering

  • Know the typical algorithms of density-based clustering

  • Understand how DBSCAN works and why density reachability plays an essential role in forming density-based cluster

  • Understand how OPTICS works and why OPTICS may lead to less requirement on parameter setting

  • Understand the basic concepts of grid-based clustering

  • Know the typical algorithms of grid-based clustering

  • Understand how STING uses a statistical information grid to do clustering

  • Understand why CLIQUE is a grid-based subspace clustering algorithm

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Density-based clustering, DBSCAN, density reachable, density-connected, OPTICS, core distance, reachability distance

  • Grid-based clustering, STING, CLIQUE, subspace clustering

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the lesson.

  • Why does density-based clustering generate arbitrary shaped clusters?

  • At what condition is density-based clustering more preferred than partitioning clustering?

  • How does DBSCAN work? Why is DBSCAN sensitive to parameter setting?

  • How does OPTICS work? In comparison to DBSCAN, what is the strength of OPTICS?

  • What is grid-based clustering? What are the typical methods of grid-based clustering?

  • How does STING use statistical information in its grid structure? How may such statistical information facilitate clustering?

  • How does CLIQUE work? Why is it a subspace clustering approach?
