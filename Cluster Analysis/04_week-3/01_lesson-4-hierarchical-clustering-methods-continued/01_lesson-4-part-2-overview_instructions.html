Lesson 4 Part 2: Hierarchical Clustering Methods (Continued)

Overview

In this lesson, you will learn the hierarchical clustering methods. We will introduce the basic concepts of hierarchical algorithms, learn several typical agglomerative and divisive hierarchical algorithms, and study several advanced hierarchical clustering methods, including BIRCH, CURE, and Chameleon. We will also learn some basic concepts for probabilistic hierarchical clustering.

Time

This lesson should take approximately two hours of dedicated time to complete, with its videos and assignments.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Understand the basic concepts of hierarchical algorithms, including typical agglomerative and divisive clustering algorithms

  • Know the strength and weakness of hierarchical clustering and why various kinds of extensions needed to overcome them

  • Understand the general framework of BIRCH, a micro-clustering-based approach to hierarchical clustering

  • Understand the general idea of CURE, which explores well-scattered representative points

  • Know the general idea of CHAMELEON, which uses graph partitioning on the KNN graph of the data

  • Understand the general idea of probabilistic hierarchical clustering

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Hierarchical clustering, dendrogram

  • Agglomerative clustering algorithm (AGNES) and divisive clustering algorithm (DIANA)

  • Similarity measures among clusters: Single link (nearest neighbor), complete link (diameter), average link (group average), and centroid link (centroid similarity)

  • Measures of a cluster: centroid, radius, and diameter

  • BIRCH, micro-clustering, clustering feature, CF tree

  • CURE (exploring well-scattered representative points)

  • CHAMELEON (graph partitioning on the KNN graph of the data)

  • Probabilistic hierarchical clustering, generative model, Gaussian distribution

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the lesson.

  • How are hierarchical clustering algorithms different from partitioning algorithms?

  • What are the major ideas of agglomerative and divisive clustering algorithms?

  • What are the major strength and weakness of hierarchical clustering algorithms?

  • How does the BIRCH algorithm work? Why is it called a micro-clustering-based approach?

  • How does the CURE algorithm work?

  • Why can CHAMELEON find better clusters than many other clustering algorithms?

  • What is probabilistic hierarchical clustering? What are its differences from typical hierarchical clustering algorithms?
