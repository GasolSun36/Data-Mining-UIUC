Lesson 2: Similarity Measures for Cluster Analysis

Overview

In this lesson, you will learn the general concepts of distance or similarity measures between objects. We will: introduce the basic concepts of similarity measure; study the concepts and formulas for computing distances on numeric data, such as Euclidean distance, Manhattan distance, and supremum distance, for computing proximity for symmetric vs. asymmetric binary variables, for computing distances between categorical attributes, ordinal attributes, and mixed types; and learn the concept and methods for computing proximity measure between two vectors, such as cosine similarity, and for computing correlation measures between two variables: covariance and correlation coefficient.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Know the basic concepts of distance or similarity measures between objects

  • Understand how to compute distance on numeric data, especially various forms of Minkowski distance, such as Euclidean distance, Manhattan distance, and Supremum distance

  • Understand how to compute proximity measure for symmetric vs. asymmetric binary variables

  • Understand the concept of distance measure between categorical attributes, ordinal attributes, and mixed types

  • Understand the concept and know how to compute proximity measure between two vectors, such as cosine similarity

  • Understand the concept and computation of correlation measures between two variables: covariance and correlation coefficient

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Similarity measure, dissimilarity measure, distance, proximity

  • Minkowski distance, Euclidean distance, Manhattan distance, supremum distance

  • Proximity measure for symmetric binary variables, distance for asymmetric binary variables, Jaccard coefficient

  • Proximity measure between two vectors, Cosine similarity

  • Correlation measures between two variables, covariance, correlation coefficient

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the lesson.

  • Why is it important to take different measures to compute similarity between objects?

  • Why is Minkowski distance a general measure of distance on numeric data? At what situation should we use Euclidean distance, Manhattan distance, or supremum distance, respectively?

  • When do we choose proximity measure for symmetric binary variables vs. asymmetric ones?

  • How do we compute distance between categorical attributes, ordinal attributes, and mixed types?

  • What is the desired proximity measure between two vectors?

  • How should we compute correlation measures between two variables? What are the differences between covariance and correlation coefficient?
