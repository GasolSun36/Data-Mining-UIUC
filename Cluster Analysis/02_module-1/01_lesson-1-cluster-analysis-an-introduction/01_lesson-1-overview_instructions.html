Lesson 1: Cluster Analysis: An Introduction

Overview

In this lesson, you will learn the general concepts of cluster analysis, understand what cluster analysis is, and understand why it is useful and important in many applications. You will also learn the basic requirements, challenges and applications of cluster analysis, get a multi-dimensional picture (categorization) of cluster analysis, understand there is a spectrum of methodologies of clustering methods, and get to know that different kinds of data may need different methodologies for cluster analysis. Further, you will see that users may have different insights on clusters to be generated, which may guide desired methods and results of cluster analysis.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Be familiar with what cluster analysis is, and why it is useful and important in many applications

  • Be familiar with the basic concepts, requirements, challenges and applications of cluster analysis

  • Learn a multi-dimensional categorization of cluster analysis

  • Understand there is a spectrum of methodologies of clustering methods

  • Understand that different kinds of data may need different methodologies for cluster analysis

  • Understand that users may have different insights on clusters to be generated which may guide desired methods and results of clustering

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Clustering, cluster analysis, data segmentation, unsupervised learning

  • Similarity measure, distance measure

  • Distance-based clustering method, density-based clustering, grid-based clustering, probabilistic clustering, generative models

  • High-dimensional clustering, subspace clustering

  • Dimensionality reduction, spectral clustering, non-negative matrix factorization

  • Constraint-based clustering, semi-supervised clustering, user-guided clustering

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the lesson.

  • What is cluster analysis? Why is cluster analysis viewed as unsupervised learning?

  • What are potential applications of cluster analysis? Under what conditions can clustering be viewed as an independent task, and under what conditions it is only part of the whole data analysis process?

  • What are the major factors to be considered in cluster analysis?

  • What are the major challenges in cluster analysis?

  • What are the multi-dimensional categorizations of cluster analysis?

  • What are the typical clustering methodologies?

  • What are the methods for clustering different types of data?

  • What is an overview of user insights and clustering?
