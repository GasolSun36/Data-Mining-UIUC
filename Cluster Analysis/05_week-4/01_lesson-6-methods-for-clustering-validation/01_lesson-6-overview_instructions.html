Lesson 6: Methods for Clustering Validation

Overview

In this lesson, you will learn clustering validation methods. We will introduce the basic concepts of clustering validation and discuss typical clustering validation measures. We first introduce external measures, such as matching-based measures, entropy-based measures, and pairwise measures. Then we introduce internal measures and relative measures for clustering validation. We will also discuss how to evaluate cluster stability and clustering tendency.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Understand the basic concepts of clustering validation and evaluation of clustering quality

  • Know the importance of evaluation of clustering quality

  • Learn a few typical external measures for clustering validation, including matching-based measures, entropy-based measures, and pairwise measures

  • Learn a few typical internal measures and relative measures for clustering validation

  • Know how to evaluate cluster stability and clustering tendency

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Clustering validation, clustering evaluation, clustering quality

  • Criteria for clustering evaluation: cluster homogeneity, cluster completeness, rag bag, small cluster preservation

  • External measures for clustering validation: Matching-based measures, entropy-based measures, pairwise measures

  • Matching-based measures: purity, maximum matching, F-measure

  • Entropy-based measures: conditional entropy, normalized mutual information (NMI)

  • Pairwise measures – Four possibilities: True positive (TP), FN, FP, TN; Jaccard coefficient

  • Internal measures for clustering validation: Beta-CV measure, normalized cu,t and modularity

  • Relative measures: Silhouette coefficient

  • Cluster stability: Finding the best k

  • Clustering tendency: Spatial histogram for clusterability assessment
