Lesson 3: Pattern Evaluation

Lesson Overview

In the lesson, you will learn how to evaluate patterns and what kind of patterns are interesting. You will see that the typical support-confidence association rule mining framework is not adequate for pattern evaluation, and even the popularly used lift and chi-square measures may not be good in some situations. We will introduce the concept of interestingness in pattern or rule evaluation, show the importance of null-invariance, and compare multiple interestingness measures.

Goals and Objectives

After you actively engage in the learning experiences in this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand that not all mined patterns under the support-confidence framework are interesting.

  • Understand that pattern evaluation will lead to the generation of a smaller set but will yield interesting and useful patterns.

  • Know that measures like lift and chi-square can be very useful in certain situations, but they may encounter difficulties in certain situations.

  • Understand the importance of null-invariance, and learn why a null-invariant measure like Kulczynski may lead to better judgement.

  • Know how to compare multiple interestingness measures.

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching the video lectures throughout the lesson.

  • Why is it that the typical support-confidence association rule mining framework may sometimes lead to misleading answers?

  • Why is it important to introduce interestingness measures in pattern evaluation?

  • In what conditions may lift and χ2 give you good answers for correlation analysis? In what conditions may it not work well?

  • What is null-invariance? Why is it important?

  • Comparing with a set of interestingness measures introduced here, which one may work well under what conditions? What is your general recommendation?
