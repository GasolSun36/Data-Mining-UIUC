Lesson 5: Sequential Pattern Mining

Lesson Overview

Many interesting applications may need to find sequential patterns (i.e., frequent subsequences) in large sequence databases. In this lesson, we will learn three efficient sequential pattern mining methods: (1) an Apriori-based sequential pattern mining method, GSP; (2) a vertical data format-based sequential pattern method, SPADE; and (3) a pattern-growth-based sequential pattern mining method, PrefixSpan. Moreover, we will learn the concept of mining closed sequential patterns and introduce an efficient closed sequential pattern mining method, CloSpan.

Goals and Objectives

After you actively engage in the learning experiences in this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand the concept of sequential patterns and sequential pattern mining, and know some typical applications of sequential pattern mining.

  • Know that the Apriori property holds for sequential patterns as well.

  • Understand the Apriori-based sequential pattern mining algorithm: GSP.

  • Know how SPADE, a sequential pattern mining algorithm in vertical data format, works.

  • Understand how PrefixSpan works.

  • Understand the concept of closed sequential patterns and how CloSpan mines such patterns.

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching the video lectures throughout the lesson.

  • Why is sequential pattern mining important in many applications?

  • How does Apriori-based sequential pattern mining work? What are its strengths and weaknesses?

  • In comparison with the horizontal data format, what are the advantages to exploring sequential pattern mining in the vertical data format?

  • How does PrefixSpan work? What are the advantages of the pattern-growth approach in sequential pattern mining?

  • Why do we want to mine closed sequential patterns? What are the major tricks that one can mine complete set of closed sequential patterns without first mining the complete set of sequential patterns?
