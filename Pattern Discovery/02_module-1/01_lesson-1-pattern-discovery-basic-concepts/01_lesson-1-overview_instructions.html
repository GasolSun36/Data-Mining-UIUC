Lesson 1: Pattern Discovery Basic Concepts

Lesson Overview

In this lesson, you will learn the general concepts of pattern discovery. We will introduce the basic concepts of frequent patterns, closed patterns, max-patterns, and association rules.

Time

This lesson should take approximately 2 hours of dedicated time to complete, with its videos and assignments.

Goals and Objectives

After you actively engage in the learning experiences in this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand what pattern discovery is, and why it is useful and important in many applications.

  • Understand the basic concepts of frequent patterns and association rules, including the basic measures such as support and confidence.

  • Understand why compressed representation of frequent patterns is an interesting idea, and what the differences are between two popular compressed representations: closed patterns and max-patterns.

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching the video lectures throughout the lesson.

  • Why is pattern discovery an important task in data mining?

  • What are the concepts of frequent itemsets?

  • What are the concepts of association rules? How do you generate association rules from frequent itemsets?

  • Why do we need compressed representations of frequent patterns? Why do we need two compression representations: closed patterns and max-patterns?

Key Phrases and Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you complete the readings and interact with the lectures. These topics will help you better understand the content in this module.

  • Patterns and pattern discovery

  • Itemsets, frequent itemsets, and frequent patterns

  • Association rules and their basic measures: support and confidence

  • Compressed representations of frequent patterns: closed patterns and max-patterns
